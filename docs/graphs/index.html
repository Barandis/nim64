6581 Audio Graphs

This is a collection of graphs of output from the 6581 SID emulation. There are several sets of graphs spread across six pages.

Each of the graphs covers 25,000 processor cycles, plotting the audio level at each cycle. Some points will of course be culled (unless you're viewing on a display that's at least 25,000 pixels wide), but this is by far the most accurate waveform views that I've been able to produce.

Type Description
Waveform generator These are outputs from the waveform generator alone at several settings. Each combination of waveforms is available at two frequencies, A4 (440 Hz) and A7 (3520 Hz). No combinations of waveforms with noise are shown because those combinations always output a steady 0.
Sync and ring modulation Additional waveform generator outputs, but these are produced by synching two generators. Three sync graphs are available per waveform (except noise, which isn't going to look much different if synched), one at A4 synched to C4 (261.6 Hz), one at A7 synched to C4, and one at A7 synched to C7 (2093 Hz). A set of three similar graphs are shown for ring-modulated triangle waves (only triangle can be ring-modulated).
Envelope generator Outputs directly from the envelope generator. There is a small variety of different settings for attack, decay, sustain, and release shown. All of them are very low settings as even an attack of 3 will last longer than 25,000 clock cycles. The graph at the bottom demonstrates the wrapping bug that happens when an attack is gated and the gate is immediately turned off; the level should stay at 0, but it instead wraps around to 255 and drops as a normal release.
Voice Outputs from the amplitude modulator that combines the waveform and envelope generator outputs. These graphs are all generated from the same envelope settings: attack 0, decay 0, sustain 8, and release 0.
Filter Outputs directly from the internal filter/mixer. The top three graphs show unmixed, unfiltered voices; the rest of the graphs show the same three waveforms combined and filtered in various ways. The low, band, and high pass filters are used with three different cutoff frequencies.
Full chip test The same graphs as with the filter, but including the external filter. This is also the only set of graphs that's produced fully through the regular pin interface without the benefit of accessing individual internal parts of the 6581. The output shown is what comes off the AUDIO pin.
